LUN Misto: where to live comfortably in Kyiv

What is it?

LUN Misto is a project of LUN that explores urban space, analyzes open data, and creates a demand for quality living in the metropolis.

The Comfort Map is a map that visualizes the results of research that helps people understand where to live comfortably in Kyiv.

Now the comfort map looks like this

Task

  1. Analyze what criteria people use to choose a comfortable place to live, how they define comfort
  2. Suggest improvements for the map by solving the problem of the multilayer filter.

For whom?

  • Kyiv residents who choose an area to buy or rent an apartment;
  • people who care about the future of Kyiv;
  • LUN users;
  • Kyiv urbanists.

User research

Interviews

To help people choose a comfortable place for living, we need to understand how they do it now and what they consider as comfortable. To do this, we conducted qualitative research, including in-depth interviews and testing of the existing solution. We translated the audio recordings into text and identified the pains, gains, and tasks that people set for themselves. This was the result of the research phase.

The artifacts were divided into 8 categories

Jobs to be done

Next, we had to turn the results of the interviews into an artifact that would help preserve and share the gained knowledge, as well as work with it further. The results of our interviews did not fit into a single scenario, there were many of them and they touched different areas of life. That's why we used Jobs to be Done.

Based on the pains, gains, and tasks we received, we generated 31 job stories. This is also a lot, so we selected 9 of them using a two-factor analysis. Each job story was rated from 0 to 10 based on two parameters: how well the need is met and how important it is to people. We selected the jobs with the most optimal ratio and generated solutions for them.

Two-factor analysis

Solutions

Testing

All the ideas were collected into one clickable prototype and tested on users. To do this, we came up with several testing scenarios. The prototype went through several iterations. The final version included the following solutions.

A character and onbirding

While testing the existing solution, we saw that it was difficult for users to navigate a large number of filters and it was unclear what information they displayed and how the map worked in general. To solve this, we added the bird Ficha, an existing character in LUN. It will accompany the user on the map and help them explore the districts of Kyiv. To do this, we drew Ficha in different situations.

Where in Kyiv is the best to...

We found out that people don't want to switch different filters as much as they want to find out a specific answer to a question "Where is best place to...?".

During the study, we identified 3 criteria or 3 types of activity that are important to people: living, resting and traveling. All the filters were divided into these three categories, reducing the workload on the user and speaking the same language with them.

Sharing is one of the indicators of project success. That's why we added a separate button for this.

3 tabs: live, rest and travel

Rating of districts

Now, to find out where in Kyiv is the best place to live, the user can select the necessary criteria and compare different districts with each other on one screen.

You can also go to the relevant studies

And what about the facilities?

Now you can see which places are nearby. There is also a night map that shows which places are open at night.

How long to work?

One of the most important criteria when choosing a place to live is how long it takes to get to and from work, how far is it to the store, is there a coffee shop nearby. We tried to make it possible to find the answers on the map: You can select a point and see how long it takes to get to other places.

In the prototype, this is shown simply as a radius, but ideally it should be shown taking into account real roads

What's next?

Chatbot

The character can act as a chatbot, answering users' questions, such as "where is the cleanest air?" or "where is the safest place to live?" This is easy to test and implement.

Engagement

In the future, users can be involved in filling out the map and making them "partners" in improving Kyiv. For example, they can enter data about neighborhoods they visit or care about.
